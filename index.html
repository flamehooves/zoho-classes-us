FERPA Compliance

FERPA rights, built into
every student interaction.

FERPA — the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (20 U.S.C. § 1232g) — gives students and parents the legal right to access their education records, request corrections, and file complaints. Any institution receiving US Department of Education funding must comply. Non-compliance risks loss of all federal funding.

Zoho Classes addresses FERPA compliance directly through its built-in Issues & Requests feature — a formal, auditable channel through which any student, parent, or faculty member can raise a request, complaint, or feedback. Every submission is tracked, timestamped, and can only be resolved by an authorised administrator. Nothing falls through the cracks.

Federal Law — 20 U.S.C. § 1232g

Students and eligible parents have the right to inspect education records, request amendments, and file complaints with the US Department of Education. Institutions must have a formal process to handle these rights. Zoho Classes Issues & Requests is that process.

"

Any vendor that receives, maintains, or accesses personally identifiable information from student education records must comply with the same data handling, disclosure, and consent requirements as the institution itself — including having a formal process to respond to student rights requests.

Source: FERPA Compliance Checklist for EdTech Vendors, 2026
